Research Experiences

I am broadly interested in designing machine learning systems that can perceive the world as we human do, which I believe is the cornerstone of achieving AGI. In my exploration of this goal, I have studied and done research in medical image analysis, efficient inference, unsupervised learning and 3D vision. I have had the great fortune to participate in several interesting projects with inspiring and talented collaborators.

During the summer of 2020, I was a summer intern researcher at Geometric Computing Group, Stanford University, where I worked with Professor Leonidas J. Guibas on the adversarial attack and defense tasks in 3D point clouds.

Prior to that, I worked on efficient inference algorithms for detection networks under the supervision of Professor Jie Zhou and Jiwen Lu in IVG. We proposed to apply binary neural networks (BNNs) for efficient object detection and leveraged the Information Bottleneck (IB) theory to improve its performance. Because of the redundancy removal introduced by the IB, we fully utilized the capacity of binary detectors and achieved SOTA on several datasets under different detection frameworks.

Previously, I had experiences in medical image segmentation and landmark regression during my first research project, advised by Professor Jianjiang Feng.
